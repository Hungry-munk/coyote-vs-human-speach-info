Coyote

by Arsam and Fabian

General Information

Coyotes are the most vocal wild canines, their speech consists of multiple complex combinations of barks, yips and howls that they use on a day-to-day basis.

Coyotes are able to produce a variety of crescendos, trills, modulations, pitches, tones, lips ( at the sides) and vocal chords. The makeup of these pattern combinations is unique to each coyote, therefore other family/ pack members can differentiate between each other by their signature howls and sounds. Coyote voices are probably as individual as our own.

How Coyote's communicate

Eleven different forms of vocalization have been identified, but more study is required to determine the precise meanings of these sounds. This includes growls, huffs, woofs, barks, bark-howls, lone howls, group yip-howls, whines, greeting songs, and yelps.

Coyotes also use body language, including gestures to convey many different messages and they mainly communicate via eye contact and gestures.

Warnings, happiness, joy, anger, distress, disapproval, pain, hellos and whereabouts are some of the things coyotes express out-loud, although the majority of communications is quiet.

What they use communication for

Mother coyotes use calls to signify their pups whether there’s danger, dinner, or other complex feelings. Their distinct yip-howl is used primarily to keep pack members in communication when separated, however it has also been used as a form of conveying a deeper emotion from the animal.

They also leave droppings and urinate on surfaces in order to notify other coyotes of their presence and their territory.

Hockett’s uniqueness features

Coyotes use some of hockets uniqueness features one being structure, coyotes use bark to convey long distance threat at a low or medium intensity, howls are used to notify their presence to listeners as well as to convey information over long distances. Coyotes developed a sound called a bark-howl, it is a blend between a barks low intensity warning and moves into a howl in the second part of the vocalization to change a less threatening warning, Into a more severe threat or alarm.
